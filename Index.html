// DIY MOD GUIDE //

Nokia 8110
Matrix Auto-Slide

The rubber band mod — as seen on HowardForums, circa 2003
⚠ WARNING: This mod involves filing down plastic parts and adding a release button hole to your phone body. Attempt only on a phone you are willing to risk. The rubber bands are not permanent — they can be removed without dismantling the phone.
// OVERVIEW

How It Works

The Nokia 8110's keypad cover slides down a pair of plastic rails on either side of the phone body. The original design requires you to push it manually. This mod puts the slider under constant tension using rubber bands, and adds a locking clip so the slide stays closed — until you press the release and it snaps open automatically.

🔴

Tension

Rubber bands hooked at 4 anchor points pull the slide downward at all times

🔒

Lock

A small spring clip catches a notch on the underside of the slide, holding it closed

⚡

Release

Pushing the clip inward unhooks it from the notch — the bands do the rest

// WHAT YOU NEED

Parts & Tools

⭕ Thin rubber bands (black recommended)
🔧 Small flat-head screwdriver
📎 Small metal spring clip (e.g. from a pen or bobby pin)
🔩 Fine metal file or sandpaper
✂️ Small drill or craft knife (for release hole)
💡 Good lighting & patience
// STEP BY STEP

The Mod

```
STEP 01

Remove & Inspect the Slide

Gently pull the keypad cover slide off the phone's rails. You'll see two long plastic tracks on either side. Look at the underside of the slide — you need to find (or file) a small notch where the locking clip will catch.

The notch must be near the top of the slide's travel — i.e. when the slide is fully closed (keypad covered), this notch lines up with where your clip will sit.

Slide removed — notch on underside
STEP 02

File the Tracks to Reduce Friction

The rubber bands won't be very strong, so you need to reduce the sliding friction as much as possible. Use a fine metal file or folded sandpaper to lightly smooth the inside faces of both plastic rail tracks on the phone body.

Don't overdo it — you just want to remove any roughness. Test the slide: it should glide with almost no resistance when you push it down with a single finger.

File the inner rail faces
STEP 03

Hook the Rubber Bands at 4 Points

This is the core of the mod. A single rubber band is hooked around 4 anchor points creating a figure-of-eight of tension:

Points 1 & 2 (TOP): Hook each end of the band around the top corners of the keypad slide itself — where the slide body has a small lip or groove.

Points 3 & 4 (BOTTOM): The band loops down and hooks around the bottom of the phone body — there are usually small raised tabs or slots near the base you can hook into.

You do not need to cut or tie the band. Just hook it. This also makes replacement easy.

Band hooked at 4 points — pulls slide DOWN
STEP 04

Add the Locking Clip

Without a lock, the slide will just fall open immediately. You need a small, springy metal clip — a straightened and re-bent hairpin, a small piece of spring steel from a pen click-mechanism, or similar.

The clip is positioned inside the phone body so that when the slide is pushed fully closed (upward), the clip's tip springs into the notch on the underside of the slide and holds it shut.

Bend the clip into an L-shape or hook shape. One end stays anchored; the other end presses outward by spring tension to catch the notch.

Clip tip catches notch to hold slide shut
STEP 05

Add the Release Button

This is the most involved part. You need a way to push the clip inward to release the notch from the outside of the phone. The original modder added a small push-button on the side or bottom of the phone.

Carefully use a small drill bit or craft knife to create a tiny hole in the phone casing, positioned so a thin tool (or your fingernail) can press the spring clip inward through the hole.

Some people use a small piece of plastic or a tiny button as a cosmetic release that pokes through this hole.

Press hole → clip releases → bands pull slide open
STEP 06

Test & Tune

Reassemble the slide onto the rails. Push it closed — you should feel the clip click into the notch. Then press the release hole. The slide should snap downward with satisfying speed.

If it's sluggish: file the rails a little more, or try tighter rubber bands. If it slams too hard: use thinner or fewer bands. If the clip doesn't catch reliably, adjust the bend angle of the clip so it has more spring tension outward.

Replace yellow or visible bands with black ones once you're happy with the mechanism — they'll be invisible and look much more factory.
